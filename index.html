A Robust ASR Domain Adaptation via LLM-based Text Augmentation Pipeline and Phonetic Respelling

[Paper]


Natsuo Yamashita, Koichi Nagatsuka, Hiroaki Kokubo, Kota Dohi, Tuan Vu Ho, Yohei Kawaguchi

Hitachi, Ltd., Japan

Abstract End-to-end automatic speech recognition often degrades on domain-specific data due to scarce in-domain resources. To address this challenge, we propose a robust domain adaptation framework that combines (i) a large language model (LLM)-based text augmentation pipeline and (ii) an LLM-based phonetic respelling method. Our pipeline produces domain-relevant texts by combining multi-stage LLM-based augmentation with a novel filtering strategy that optimizes lexical diversity, perplexity, and domain-specific vocabulary weighting. The phonetic respelling method introduces pronunciation variability directly at the text generation stage, simulating a wider range of realistic speech variations for synthetic speech. Experimental results across multiple domain-specific datasets demonstrate that the proposed methods consistently improve recognition accuracy.

This page is for research demonstration purposes only, which provides prompt examples, generated texts and synthesized audio with proposed methods.

Overview

LLM-based text augmentation pipeline first over-generates a large pool of candidate sentences (steps 1-5: domain seed, context seed, multilingual prompting, paraphrasing, multiple LLMs) and then applies filtering (step 6) which optimizes TTR, perplexity, and domain-specific terms weighting.

LLM-based phonetic respelling rewrites text with LLMs into alternative spellings that approximate natural spoken pronunciation, introducing realistic pronunciation variability without relying on phonetic symbols.
