Robust ASR Domain Adaptation via LLM-based Text Augmentation and Phonetic Respelling

Natsuo Yamashita, Koichi Nagatsuka, Hiroaki Kokubo, Kota Dohi, Tuan Vu Ho, Yohei Kawaguchi
Hitachi, Ltd., Japan

Abstract. End-to-end automatic speech recognition often degrades on domain-specific data due to scarce in-domain resources. To address this challenge, we propose a domain adaptation framework that combines (i) a large language model (LLM)-based text augmentation pipeline and (ii) an LLM-based phonetic respelling method. Our pipeline produces domain-relevant texts by multi-stage augmentation with a novel filtering strategy optimizing lexical diversity, perplexity, and domain-specific vocabulary coverage. The phonetic respelling method introduces pronunciation variability at the text generation stage, simulating a wider range of realistic speech variations for synthetic speech. Experimental results across multiple domain-specific datasets demonstrate that the proposed methods consistently improve recognition accuracy.

Overview

Our approach consists of an LLM-based text augmentation pipeline and a phonetic respelling strategy for improving ASR in specific domains. The text augmentation pipeline generates diverse in-domain text using multiple prompting and translation steps, then filters them to maximize lexical diversity and include important domain terms. The phonetic respelling introduces realistic pronunciation variations by converting text into how it might sound (using regular letters instead of phonetic symbols), which helps the ASR model handle spoken variations. The figure below illustrates the pipeline and an example of phonetic respelling.

Phonetic Respelling Example

For example, the sentence:

Original: “Zhang Feng piloted the Boeing Seven Three Seven aircraft.”

can be respelled as:

Respelling: “Jang Feng pilotid ze Bo-in Sevem Three Sevem eer-kraft.”

This respelled sentence uses English letters to mimic how the words sound (capturing accents, mispronunciations, or idiosyncrasies), introducing pronunciation variability for training.
