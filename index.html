Demo: A Robust ASR Domain Adaptation via LLM-based Text Augmentation Pipeline and Phonetic Respellings

Authors: Natsuo Yamashita, Koichi Nagatsuka, Hiroaki Kokubo, Kota Dohi, Tuan Vu Ho, Yohei Kawaguchi

Affiliation: Hitachi, Ltd., Japan

Abstract

End-to-end automatic speech recognition (ASR) systems often suffer from performance degradation when applied to domain-specific data due to scarce in-domain resources. To address this challenge, we propose a robust domain adaptation framework that combines (i) a large language model (LLM)-based text augmentation pipeline and (ii) an LLM-based phonetic respelling method. Our pipeline produces domain-relevant texts by combining multi-stage LLM-based augmentation with a novel filtering strategy that optimizes lexical diversity, perplexity, and domain-specific vocabulary weighting. The phonetic respelling method introduces pronunciation variability directly at the text generation stage, simulating a wider range of realistic speech variations for synthetic speech. Experimental results across multiple domain-specific datasets demonstrate that the proposed methods consistently improve recognition accuracy.
